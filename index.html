Assistant Project Scientist, UC Berkeley

I develop mathematical models and computational tools for the genetic control of mosquito vectors and the diseases they transmit. Previously, I worked at the Helmholtz Institute for Functional Marine Biodiversity (University of Oldenburg) and the Max Planck Institute for Evolutionary Biology. My interests span gene drives, eco‑evolutionary dynamics, microbial competition, evolutionary game theory, and optimal control.

At a glance

  • Research areas: gene drive dynamics, resistance evolution, mating systems, network effects.
  • Methods: dynamical systems, stochastic & spatial models, simulation, optimal control.
  • Applications: vector‑borne disease control, agriculture, conservation.

Optimal Control in Hybrid Ecological‑Economic Systems

Identifying optimal policies to protect ecosystems that provide critical services when subject to abrupt regime shifts and uncertainty; integrates Value‑of‑Information into dynamic decision‑making.

Gene Drive

Modelling the spread, confinement, and resistance evolution of synthetic gene drives; assessing ecological and regulatory risks through deterministic, stochastic, and spatial models.

Microbial Competition

Spatial models of antibiotic‑producing, non‑producing, and sensitive strains to understand coexistence conditions and evolution of production rates.

Bribery Games

Evolutionary game‑theoretic analysis of harassment bribery on inter‑dependent networks; interplay of topology, connectivity, and update rules.
